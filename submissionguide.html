Submission Guidelines

Write the author/authors' name, affiliation, and the correspondence email under the Title.

Please follow the guidelines for your abstract

  • The abstracts should be prepared in English. Abstracts can only be submitted for oral/paper/poster presentations in MS Word Document.
  • The abstract must not exceed 250 words.
  • The abstract should clearly state the hypothesis and major objective(s) of the research. The work must be completely original and not previously published or presented [AI generated abstract/ideas are discouraged].
  • Abstract Title: Single paragraph, Times New Roman, 12pt, regular, left-aligned with 1.5-line spacing
  • Title: Centrally aligned, bold, 12pt font
  • Key words: Give 4-5 keywords at the end of the abstract in alphabetical order separated with semicolons, not included in the title.
  • Referencing: APA Style, 7th Edition.
  • Abstract format sample: Link

Accepted abstracts will be published in the abstract book of the conference.

Conference Themes

The scope of AICRAL 2025 includes areas of Linguistics, Applied Linguistics and Language Practices. Abstracts for papers and posters, for AICRAL 2025 are invited in the following areas:

  • Artificial Intelligence in Language Education
  • CALL and Language Research
  • Corpus Linguistics
  • Digital Tools for Language Research
  • Machine Translation
  • Multimodal Media Studies
  • Computational Forensic Approaches
  • NLP Innovations for Cultural Heritage Collections
  • Language Documentation and Preservation
  • Sentiment Analysis in Language and Communication Studies
  • Language and Migration
  • Education, Research, and Globalization
  • Gender and Equality in Education
  • Inclusive Learning and Assistive Technologies
  • Rethinking Assessment in the Age of AI
  • Curriculum and Instructional Design
  • Ecolinguistics and Climate Change
  • Contemporary Linguistics
  • Diversity, Multiculturalism and Inclusion
